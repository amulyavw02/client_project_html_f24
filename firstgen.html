First Generation of UofM

Michigan is our Home

At the University of Michigan, undergraduates and graduate students are considered first-generation college students if neither parent or guardian has completed a 4-year college or university degree.

Resources for Your Success

At the University of Michigan, we offer a range of specialized programs designed to support first-generation students. These resources aim to help you adjust to university life, connect with other first-gen students, and achieve your personal and academic goals. Here are some of the key resources available to you:

  • First-Generation Student Network: Join a community of first-generation students, participate in events, and access resources that help you feel at home on campus.
  • Academic Mentoring: Receive personalized guidance from mentors who understand the first-generation experience and can provide advice on navigating academics, time management, and more.
  • Financial Aid Assistance: Get help understanding financial aid options, scholarships, and grants specifically available to first-generation students to support your educational journey.
  • Leadership Development Programs: Participate in programs that build your leadership skills, helping you become a confident leader both on campus and beyond.
  • Career Counseling Services: Access resources and workshops that help you explore career paths, develop professional skills, and prepare for internships and job searches.
